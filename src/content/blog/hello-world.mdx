Ten years into offensive security, most of what I've learned lives in engagement reports,
private notes, and Slack threads. Time to change that.

## Why I'm writing

- **Sharpen the craft.** Writing forces you to actually understand something.
- **Give back.** I learned this trade from people who published freely. Fair trade.
- **A durable record.** So next time I see the same technique, past-me has already done the homework.

## What you'll find here

- Red team writeups (cloud, AD, web)
- Tooling notes: what I use, what I'd build differently, what I wish existed
- Threat modeling and design reviews from the other side of the table
- Occasional deep dives when a bug or technique deserves one

## Ground rules

- **No war stories that would identify a client.** Ever. Sanitized, generalized, or lab-reproduced only.
- **Show, don't just tell.** If a technique matters, it comes with a lab and code.
- **Be honest about mistakes.** The interesting learning is in what didn't work.
